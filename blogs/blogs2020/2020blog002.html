for leon (extended)

i will never forget you~
RIP

Posted by Aaric Han on December 17, 2020

prologue

Leon Gu was an amazing person you could rely on, joke around with, and be happy he's your friend. I was extremely devastated to hear of his passing. He was a role model to everyone and someone I believed would have an extremely successful future. He didn't deserve to leave like this and even though he probably thought it would be okay, he was wrong. It wasn't okay. He had no right to leave us this early. He had no right to leave.


for leon

As a poet once said: “It takes an hour to like someone…but it takes a lifetime to forget someone…”

Leon, you were always one of the only people in the Wind Ensemble clarinet section that wasn’t a clown during class. The effort and time you put into clarinet made everyone feel like we weren’t practicing nearly enough. Sadly, we never got to compete with one another in All-District and All-State Band. I always worry about what would happen if we were in the same grade group. Your infectious work ethic brought up many admirers. Your radiant smile and consoling words could lift everyone’s spirits no matter what happened in our lives. When I had a horrible morning before band class and vented my frustrations, you looked at me with a look of understanding and sympathy and told me that I would be fine. You were always so generous to share with or help anyone with anything; and, when it came time to repay your kindness, you would respond with a simple: “It’s fine” and a smile. I learned a lot about what it means to be a better person from you. Thank you for being here with me.

A semester is a relatively short time to have known you, but it will take more than a lifetime to forget you. These moments we shared will forever be in my memories. Until we meet again~


epilogue

I hate how I knew that people from Panther Creek High School would apply for Duke University Early Decision and on December 18th, 2020 at 7pm, I didn't reach out to anyone on Instagram asking if they got in. If I did, maybe Leon would have responded and he wouldn't be gone. I had a chance to alter what happened but I didn't take that chance. I hate myself for it. I hate myself. I hate myself! I hate myself!!

If you are reading this, please understand that if you are happy, someone else isn't. It's our job to reach out and check up on our friends especially when you feel like they might have everything in the world. If you are reading this and you aren't happy yourself, please remember to talk with other people about your feelings. Any problems you have can be solved. Just like any math problem can be solved. All it takes is more heads to work together and more time.

I learned a lot from personal experiences and the recent tragedy of Leon. Here are two of the ones that will always remember:

  1. Smart people are more likely to hide their emotions well. Even though they seem happy, they might not be on the inside.
  2. Always check up on people you care about. You never know when the most recent happy conversation you share turns into the last one.
